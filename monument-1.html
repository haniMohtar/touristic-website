Fushimi Inari Shrine

About the Monument

Fushimi Inari Taisha is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings. The shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Shinto god of rice, and is located in southern Kyoto, Japan.

History

Established in 711 AD, it is one of the oldest Shinto shrines in Japan. The trails up the mountain feature over 10,000 torii gates donated by individuals and businesses over the years.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Kyoto, Japan
  • Opening Hours: 24 Hours
  • Entrance Fee: Free
  • Best Time: Early Morning
