Stonehenge

About the Monument

Stonehenge is perhaps the world's most famous prehistoric monument. Located on the Salisbury Plain in Wiltshire, England, it consists of a ring of standing stones, each around 13 feet high, seven feet wide, and weighing approximately 25 tons. The stones are set within earthworks in the middle of the most dense complex of Neolithic and Bronze Age monuments in England, including several hundred burial mounds

History

Archaeologists believe Stonehenge was constructed in several stages from 3000 BC to 2000 BC. The surrounding circular earth bank and ditch, which constitute the earliest phase of the monument, have been dated to about 3100 BC. While the exact purpose of the site remains a mystery, it is widely believed that it served as a burial ground and a sophisticated astronomical calendar. The stones are perfectly aligned with the sunrise of the summer solstice and the sunset of the winter solstice.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Wiltshire, England
  • Built: 3000 BC - 2000 BC
  • Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Entrance Fee: Paid: £25 - £28.50
  • Fun Fact: The smaller "bluestones" were transported from Wales, over 150 miles away!
