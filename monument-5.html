Pyramids of Giza

About the Monument

The Pyramids of Giza, located on the Giza Plateau near Cairo, Egypt, are the most iconic symbols of ancient Egyptian civilization. The complex includes the Great Pyramid of Giza (built for Pharaoh Khufu), the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure, along with the Great Sphinx. The Great Pyramid is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and the only one to remain largely intact. It was the tallest man-made structure in the world for more than 3,800 years.

History

Constructed during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, these massive structures were built as royal tombs. The Great Pyramid alone consists of an estimated 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing several tons. The precision with which they were built continues to baffle modern engineers; the base of the Great Pyramid is level to within 15 millimeters, and its sides are aligned almost exactly to the four cardinal points of the compass. The internal chambers, including the King's and Queen's chambers, were designed to protect the Pharaoh's mummified body and his possessions for the afterlife.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Giza, Greater Cairo, Egypt
  • Built: ~2580-2560 BC (Old Kingdom)
  • Status: The only remaining "Ancient Wonder"
  • Construction: Estimated 2.3 million stone blocks
  • Entrance Fee: Paid
